######## Findings
The EU AI Act and the U.S. Executive Order on AI differ significantly in their regulatory approach to General Purpose AI (GPAI) models, particularly in terms of legal enforceability, scope, risk thresholds, and mechanisms for compliance. While both aim to promote safe and responsible AI development, the EU adopts a binding, risk-based regulatory framework with specific obligations for high-impact models, whereas the U.S. relies on executive directives, voluntary cooperation, and federal coordination without immediate legislative force.
